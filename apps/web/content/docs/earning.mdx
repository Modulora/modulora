## Earning during alpha

- **Profit share**: your free, open components earn a share of Modulora's
  distributable profit based on how often they're actually installed — no
  listing required.
- **External sales**: you may link a listing to a purchase page on a domain
  you have verified. You handle checkout, licensing, delivery, and support;
  Modulora records no purchase and takes no fee.

Direct checkout for components through Modulora is shelved for alpha. The code
may return later behind a feature flag after buyer and seller flows are ready.

## The split

Of Modulora's distributable profit for a period, **30% goes to creators, 10%
to an open-source fund, and 60% is retained by Modulora** to run and grow the
platform.

*Distributable profit* means revenue in the profit-share pool minus the costs
of earning it — payment processing fees, infrastructure, and operating costs
including salaries. It's what's left to distribute, not gross revenue.
**Modulora Plus subscription revenue joins this pool** — subscribing directly
supports creators of free components.

The 30% creator pool is divided among creators in proportion to their
verified installs for that period.

## What counts — verified installs only

Only **digest-verified installs through the Modulora CLI** count toward your
share. Each install receipt is tied to the exact published content hash.

Page views, likes, or any number we can't independently verify never affect
earnings. This is the same honesty rule as the rest of Modulora: we don't pay
on things we can't prove. Installs of components that violate the publishing
policy, or that we can't attribute to a real install, are excluded.

## Getting paid

Connect a Stripe account once (we handle verification, banking, and tax
through Stripe). Earnings accrue per period and distributions pay out once
your accrued share reaches **$25**; smaller balances roll over — nothing is
forfeited.

You keep ownership of your components and their licenses. Participating in
profit share doesn't change your license or transfer your rights. Reversed
payments, fraud, or policy violations can reduce or claw back the
corresponding earnings.

## Fair and transparent

We publish the split and the definition of distributable profit, and we never
sell rank, trust, or moderation — earnings can't be bought.

The binding terms are the Creator Earnings Terms; this page is a
plain-language summary. If they ever conflict, the Terms govern.
