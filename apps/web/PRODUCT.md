# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Modulora primarily serves component consumers and coding agents evaluating, installing, and verifying reusable UI across supported frameworks. React is the launch framework, not the boundary of the product; the registry and specification are intended to expand to Vue, Svelte, and others.

Component creators are the essential secondary audience. They need to publish, distribute, sell, and measure adoption without surrendering ownership, attribution, licensing control, or the value their work creates.

Established creators are also reputation-sensitive participants. Listing on Modulora can imply association even when it costs nothing. They need confidence that the surrounding catalog will not normalize plagiarism, impersonation, unattributed derivatives, or low-effort copies of their work—and they must remain fully in control of whether their name, brand, or components appear.

## Product Purpose

Modulora is an open, provenance-first component registry. It helps builders find suitable UI, understand its source, evidence, license, and fulfillment model, then install the exact files they intended to install. It helps creators distribute the same work on fair terms through free listings, direct marketplace sales, external fulfillment, collections, and profit sharing.

Success is reciprocal: builders complete confident installs, creators retain control and earn sustainably, and the exchange stays healthy rather than optimizing one side at the expense of the other. Success also means a reputable creator can associate with Modulora without lending credibility to plagiarized work elsewhere in the catalog.

## Positioning

Modulora makes component distribution fair: builders get inspectable, attributable UI while creators retain ownership, control, reputation, and a share of the value their work creates.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, bold, and defiant. Modulora should challenge opaque marketplaces and extractive distribution without becoming noisy, speculative, or careless. Its voice is direct and specific: strong claims are backed by exact records, and limitations are stated plainly.

## Anti-references

Modulora must not resemble a generic SaaS template. Avoid interchangeable dashboard composition, decorative purple gradients, glass-card surfaces, vague AI-era copy, inflated claims, and visual polish that hides weak information hierarchy. It must also avoid generic “verified” trust theater: trust is always scoped to the evidence actually collected.

Modulora must not resemble marketplaces where catalog growth outruns authorship integrity. Avoid anonymous reposts, ambiguous attribution, silent forks, copied previews, weak impersonation controls, and review language that implies originality was proven when it was not. Similarity detection is a review signal, not proof of plagiarism or proof of originality. “Everything is reviewed” must always mean a documented listing review with an explicit scope—not a security, ownership, or originality guarantee.

## Design Principles

1. **Make the exchange legible.** Show builders what they receive and creators what they retain, earn, and control.
2. **Put evidence beside decisions.** Provenance, license, pricing, fulfillment, and limitations belong where users evaluate or install—not in a distant trust center.
3. **Challenge through specificity.** Be bold by naming exact files, records, terms, and tradeoffs rather than adding visual noise or combative slogans.
4. **Never sell trust.** Promotion can change placement, but never evidence, review priority, approval, or perceived safety.
5. **Design beyond the launch framework.** React-specific implementation may lead v0, but product language and information architecture should remain framework-extensible.
6. **Protect creator association.** A creator's identity, brand, source, previews, and external links are never claimed, imported, or presented as affiliated without authorization. Make ownership claims, original URLs, similarity signals, reports, disputes, review history, and takedown status legible.
7. **Treat plagiarism as an adversarial risk.** Similarity systems and human review reduce risk but cannot eliminate it. Design for prevention, accountable review, fast reporting, evidence preservation, appeals, and decisive remediation instead of promising an impossible plagiarism-free catalog.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA across public and authenticated surfaces. Support complete keyboard operation, visible focus states, reduced-motion preferences, sufficient text and control contrast, accessible names, and status communication that never relies on color alone. Evidence and trust language must remain understandable without specialized security knowledge.

Reporting, dispute, and takedown flows must be accessible to creators who do not hold a Modulora account. They must use plain language, preserve due process for both parties, and never require a public confrontation to challenge attribution or association.
